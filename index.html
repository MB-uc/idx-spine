01 — The problem

The content exists. The AI cannot read it.

AI platforms — ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Copilot — have become a primary channel through which investors, analysts, job seekers, and procurement managers seek corporate information. AI referral traffic across the IDX portfolio has grown 240-fold since January 2024.

But the most-visited sections of major corporate websites are frequently the least represented in AI-generated answers. Careers, supplier onboarding, IR tools, deep product pages — high human traffic, near-zero AI penetration. The content exists. The problem is structure.

The semantic spine is the solution to that structural problem.

02 — The mechanism

How a spine node becomes machine-readable content

01
NODE
Spine node definition
A precisely governed concept. T1.1 — Job listings and search: the primary interface through which candidates discover open roles. One definition, one meaning, across every client.
02
TYPE
schema.org mapping
Each node carries a schema.org type. T1.1 maps to schema.org/JobPosting. The type is the machine-readable vocabulary that search engines and AI platforms understand.
03
{JSON}
JSON-LD in page HTML
The Schema Controller writes a JSON-LD script block to the page's <head>. The block contains the structured properties the AI platform needs to classify and cite the content.
04
AI ↗
AI can read and cite it
The AI platform parses the structured data, classifies the page, and can answer: "What engineering roles does Anglo American have in Chile?" — with current, authoritative, citable content.

With the spine — T1.1 JobPosting schema Node T1.1 · Critical · schema.org/JobPosting 
<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "JobPosting",
  "title": "Executive Head of Technical and Strategy",
  "hiringOrganization": {
    "@type": "Organization",
    "name": "Anglo American",
    "sameAs": "https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Q47876"
  },
  "jobLocation": {
    "@type": "Place",
    "address": {
      "addressCountry": "ZA",
      "addressLocality": "Johannesburg"
    }
  },
  "employmentType": "FULL_TIME",
  "datePosted": "2026-04-01",
  "validThrough": "2026-05-01"
}
</script>

// AI can now answer: "What engineering roles does
// Anglo American have?" — with current, citable data.

04 — The opportunity

What changes when the spine is in place

Each node carries a schema.org mapping, an AEO priority rating, and a freshness threshold. When the infrastructure is in place, AI representation assessment becomes a repeatable managed service — not a series of one-off audits.

27
ESG nodes
From climate targets to supply chain transparency. The domain with the highest organic AI penetration — up to 1.37% without schema intervention. With it: structured, citable data points for every ESG framework disclosure.
25
IR nodes
Results, governance, capital allocation, dividends. Currently suppressed by JS-rendered tools and PDF-first publishing. HTML-first publishing + schema.org/FinancialProduct and Report types unlock the gap.
20
CC nodes
Press releases, leadership, corporate narrative, supplier onboarding. CC6.1 — Supplier engagement — is a portfolio-derived node: no external standard covers it. IDX is the originating authority.
18
CT nodes
The highest-traffic, lowest-AI-penetration domain. Job listings are verified JavaScript-rendered shells with no structured data. schema.org/JobPosting on T1.1 unlocks 21.8M annual sessions currently invisible to AI.
17
BP nodes
Brand portfolio, product catalogue, innovation pipeline, operations. B2.2 — Product innovation — achieves 0.85% AI penetration organically at Rolls-Royce. Named programme schema would push that further.
5
Portfolio-derived nodes
E2.1, E2.4, E3.5, E3.6, E4.4 — originated by IDX from portfolio traffic evidence. No external standard covers these concepts at this specificity. These are IDX's original contribution to the vocabulary of corporate digital communication.

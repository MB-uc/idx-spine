Engagement quality
Volume tells one story. How long visitors stay tells another.

Average engagement time per visit has held steady at 18–22 seconds across the full period — surprisingly consistent given the 240× volume growth. But domain-level data reveals a significant divergence: IR visitors are spending just 11–16 seconds per page (scan-and-retrieve), while CC, ESG, and CT visitors spend 20–25 seconds (sustained reading). The spine's behaviour archetypes — BHV-001 through BHV-005 — map directly to these patterns.

Engagement held stable despite 240× volume growth
Portfolio avg engagement per visit ranged from 17.0s to 27.5s across 27 months with no structural trend. Volume scaled without degrading session quality — the channel matured, it did not commoditise.
IR visitors are scanners; ESG and CC visitors are readers
IR avg 11–16s per page (BHV-005: scan-and-retrieve — finding the document and leaving). CC and ESG avg 20–25s (BHV-001: sustained reading — building understanding). Careers (CT) shows a divergence in Q1 2026 — see below.
CT engagement collapsed in Q1 2026 despite volume growth
CT pageviews grew 17.6% post-peak — but avg secs per page dropped from 24s to 9s in Feb–Mar 2026. Growing CT volume appears to be shallower browse traffic, not deep reading. Quantity is up; quality needs watching.
Distinct users growing faster than visits in Q1 2026
25,002 distinct users in March 2026 from 69,426 visits — users as % of visits holding at ~36%. Site penetration (104 sites) still expanding while visit volumes plateau. New sites, not more visits per site.

The semantic spine
105 canonical nodes mapped to the five domains driving this traffic

A governed controlled vocabulary covering the content types that generate AI referral traffic across the portfolio. Every node carries an AEO priority rating grounded in traffic evidence — and now a behaviour archetype grounded in engagement data. Select a domain to see its node inventory.

The spine names what the traffic data shows. Two years of AI referral data across 125 sites points to the same five content domains. The engagement data adds a second dimension: not just which domains are queried, but how. IR is queried by scanners retrieving documents. ESG and CC are read by analysts building understanding. Careers is browsed by candidates making employer decisions. The spine governs all of it — and the behaviour archetypes embedded in every node reflect exactly these patterns.

Every node carries the evidence that justifies its priority rating. Audiences, sectors, engagement behaviours, and temporal cycles — all derived from the portfolio data IDX already holds. The AEO service line is the delivery mechanism. The spine is the intelligence layer that tells it what to prioritise, and why.

That kind of cross-portfolio intelligence takes a decade to build. Not a funding round.
